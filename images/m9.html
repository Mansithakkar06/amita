Memory foam mattresses tend to be soft and supportive. They can reduce body pressure and conform to a person’s unique shape. This may reduce pain and help a person get better sleep.

What to look for in a memory foam mattress

People may wish to consider the following when choosing a memory foam mattress:

  • Certifications: People may wish to look for mattresses that have certifications from organizations such as CertiPUR-US and GREENGUARD Gold. These certifications mean that the mattresses are free from materials or compounds that are hazardous to human health.
  • Cooling features: Memory foam is denser than other mattress materials and may sleep hotter. People may wish to choose a product that has cooling features such as cooling gel to avoid overheating during sleep.
  • Firmness: Firmness is subjective, and people should always choose the firmness that feels most comfortable. That said, most people will find a medium-firm mattress suitable to sleep on.
  • Height: Mattresses with more height tend to have extra layers, which may provide more comfort. However, people who may find it difficult to sit on a bed or to get out of bed may prefer to choose a thinner mattress.
  • Size: People should always purchase a mattress that is the same size as their bedframe.
  • Warranties and sleep trials: People may wish to purchase from companies that offer more generous sleep trials and warranty lengths.
  • Price: Memory foam mattresses can be expensive. People may wish to shop within their budget.

Benefits of memory foam mattresses

Memory foam is a relatively new mattress material, so not many studies have investigated its impact on sleep quality or back pain.

One 2018 study compared memory foam mattresses with other types of mattress and found that participants using the memory foam mattress reported better sleep and less body pressure.

However, a 2014 analysisTrusted Source of previous research found no statistically significant differences between a memory foam-like mattress, a foam mattress without springs, and a waterbed for people with chronic back pain.

A person’s health needs change over time, and what works for one person may not work for another. When choosing a mattress, it is a good idea to consider factors such as body weight, any injuries or joint conditions, age, and climate to find the best option.

Other types of mattresses 

There are many other types of mattress on the market. Some other popular mattress types include:

  • innerspring mattresses, which contain steel coils surrounded by layers of cotton or stuffing
  • pillow-top mattresses, which have a soft, pillowy layer on top
  • hybrid mattresses, which blend newer technologies such as memory foam and gel with a traditional spring core
  • airbeds, which feature pockets filled with air
  • waterbeds, which are filled with water and create the sensation of floating

Each type has its pros and cons, so it is worth looking into what each can offer.

Organic and natural mattresses can also be better for the environment, which is another consideration for many consumers.

Frequently asked questions about memory foam mattresses

Here we answer some common questions about these products.

When should I replace my memory foam mattress?

People should replace their mattress when they notice it does not give them enough support. This may mean people wake up with aches and pains or notice sagging or soft spots on the mattress.

Can memory foam mattresses go on slats?

Yes, people can place memory foam mattresses on slats.

However, the slats should be close together. Slats that are not close together may lead to the memory foam mattress sagging into the spaces.

Are memory foam mattresses too hot?

Memory foam mattresses can be denser than other types of mattresses as they do not contain springs that allow extra airflow. Therefore, memory foam mattresses may sleep hotter.

However, people can choose mattresses that contain cooling technology such as cooling gel, which may prevent overheating.

Summary

What makes a mattress the best memory foam mattress depends on a person’s sleep preferences, how much support they like to have, whether or not they tend to overheat, and other considerations.

To find the right fit, a person can take note of their personal needs and make use of risk-free trials that many mattress companies offer. This can help them test out the mattress to see if it helps them sleep well.
